About

Hi, I'm Glen's website!

Glen started his career in 3D printing, but after spending more and more time tinkering with firmware and printer hosts he moved into software development. First in contract roles, then full time at Zippity, as part of a two person dev team. From there he spent 4 years at Amazon working to internalize their HR software. He then left, intending to renovate his home full time, but ended up contracting with local businesses to digitize and automate back of house workflows. With the home renovation winding down and running out of local businesses to help, he's looking for a new full time role.

Home Renovation

My wife and I moved into a house in disrepair during COVID. Contractors in our area were booked out for a couple years and material prices were spiking. We decided to wait for materials cost to come down and do the work ourselves.

The renovation has taken our home down to the studs, and beyond, where the studs were rotted out. Suprisingly similar to upgrading legacy software...

Dissectables

I was the principal investigator for an NSF research grant. We were assessing the viability of producing surgical simulations using inexpensive FFF printing methods.

This required designing a printer that could easily swap out large experimental extruders in order to print with cutting edge filaments that were not useable by any off the shelf printers.

This is when I learned that cadaver labs were not for me and that hearts have tendons.

OC3D

Aftering assisting several faculty members with 3D printing, I was invited to start a 3D printing lab, where I set up printers, held open hours, and ran a winter study. The students built even more printers, including a ceramic printer, along with too many other projects to name.
